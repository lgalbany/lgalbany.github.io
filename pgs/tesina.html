A CCD is simply an array of MOS capacitors.

These capacitors are grouped in threes forming pixels.

The DES CCDs are made by an n-type silicon substrate, a p-type epitaxial layer, a SiO2 dielectric layer, and a polysilicon gates.

Two depletion regions are created: the junction one biasing the substrate, and the surface one biasing the gates

The region between them where the charge can be accumulated is called channel

Biasing the gates with different voltages, we can create potential wells where the charge can be accumulated

CLICK

When a photon interacts with the silicon, an electron is pulled out from the atom and is moved by the electric field to the back of the substrate

The hole is rapidly filled by electrons, generating a net movement of the hole to the channel

CLICK

Once in the channel, the hole is moved to the region of low potential

 

16

 

When we have to read the information we move the charge collected to the next capacitor playing with the voltages

First we decrease the voltage of the next gate in order to share the charge with the next capacitor

Then we increase the voltage of the original well.

This process has to take place three times to move the charge along one pixel

 

17

 

This is a scheme of a CCD.

The yellow part is the array of MOS capacitors, isolated by columns

In this region the charge transfer is performed vertically to the serial register, in red.

CLICK

This array of capacitors can move the charge horizontally to the amplifier that converts the charge in digital signal, called ADUs

In the serial register there are two amplifiers, which read each half part of the CCD

When the last row in the serial register s totally read more clocks are done in order to generate more pixels in the image, the overscan region

Here we can see a typical image, where there are the two half parts of the CCD and more measurements of the empty wells

When the CCD is cooled and at the vacuum desired, automatically MecStart starts to take the sequence of images needed to perform all the tests.

826 images are needed to totally characterize each CCD

When MecStart finishes, a Linux script distribute the images to different folders and execute the analysis tools.

Useful regions of the images are selected and converted to text files with the number of

ADU of the pixels

The selected regions are computed and the results of the analysis of each test are plotted using ROOT

CLICK

The first test is the OTG test, a preliminary test to determine if the CCD is working as an image sensor or as a memory device.

The thickness of the well can be measured as an effective potential.

We can measure it fixing the reference voltage and increasing the voltage applied to the gates.

When the channel produces a potential enough deep to meet the two depletion regions, there is no channel, and this voltage is the gate voltage at which the charge injection occurs.

We will study this voltage using the last capacitor of the serial register called OTG.

We will take 1 clear and 20 sec exposed images for each pair of values, Votg from 0 to 9V, and Vref from -12 to -6V

From the images we will select this red region from the overscan region and compute the mean signal for each channel

The next is the PTC test, with it we can measure many parameters:

The signal measured in a pixel is a result of many processes:

Defining K as the number of electrons needed to produce one ADU, we can measure it, computing the variance of the exposed region as a function of its mean value

We have done twice this sequence for each exposure time from 1 to 40 seconds

Plotting then the mean signal value as a function of exposure time, we can find the other parameters

 

25

 

These are the plots automatically generated.

We plot the mean signal of the exposed images in green or each exposure time

And we do the same for the nonexposed in blue

The difference between them is also plotted in red.

The pedestal can be measured as the signal point of the nonexposed fit that intercepts the y-axis, because is the charge that would be in a 0seconds exposed image

The dark current is the increment in ADU of the nonexposed images

The linearity is the difference between the points plotted and the fit, which has to be lower than 1% to pass the DES requirements

With the red points we plot its variance as a function of the mean value

The well capacity is the signal value where the mean values saturate

And finally the gain is the slope of these measurements

 

26

 

The next test is the muon test, which is an alternative way to measure the gain of the CCD

Muon is a MIP, and it is known how many charge deposits when it crosses a detector

In silicon this loss is of 74 electrons per micron

As we have pixels of 15micron, we expect to have 1100 electrons more in the pixels where the muon has crossed, but it can depend on the incident angle of the muon

We analyze the longer traces, because we can assume that the 3rd dimension can not be taken into account.

We take 100 images of 15 seconds but only some of them are useful, because the interaction is random

In order to measure the signal deposited, we subtract from the image, a master dark, the same image without traces

 

27

 

The NOISE test is a very simple test

As it is different to disentangle, we compute 1 measure of the noise, taking into account the thermal noise, the electronics readout system noise, and the shot noise, the fluctuations related to take to different measurements

We simply take 10 consecutive readings and measure the RMS of the signal distribution in the overscan region

Then we average the result of the 10 images

The DES requirement is to achieve less than 15 electrons, this CCD would pass the requirements

 

28

 

The CTI test is performed in order to check the both proper operation of the potential wells, and the transfer efficiency.

This test has 4 subtests, one for every value related to the transfer process

I said before we apply 2 different voltages at the gates in a pixel to create the wells

We select the regions of the transition to overscan region

We compute the mean signal for each column, the mean signal of the yellow exposed area, and the mean signal of the orange pedestal area.

What we do is normalize the mean signal per columns to the mean signal of the exposed area

Then we measure the CTI dividing the normalized signal in the first column of the overscan region by the number of transfers its charge has to suffer before arrive to the sense node

In each image we have also measured the noise, as before

We take 1 clear and a 10sec exposed image fixing three of the voltages and varying the other one in each test. The positives from 1 to 10.5V, and the negatives from -10 to -0.5

 

29

 

In this H+ example, we can see that the higher the voltage is, the better the transfer is performed, and this is due to the deep of the wells

Measuring the CTI we can see that at some value of H+ the requirement is achieved, 10-5
